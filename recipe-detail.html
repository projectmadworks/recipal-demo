The Perfect Cast Iron Ribeye

A restaurant-quality crust without the grill. Butter-basted, herb-finished, and done in under 20 minutes. The most important thing here is a hot pan and a dry steak.

    Instructions
    1. 01
      Dry and season the steak
      Pat the ribeye completely dry with paper towels — moisture is the enemy of a good crust. Season generously with salt and pepper on all sides, including the edges. Let it sit at room temperature for at least 20 minutes before cooking.
    2. 02
      Get the pan smoking hot
      Heat your cast iron skillet over high heat for 3–4 minutes until it's genuinely smoking. Add the oil and swirl. You want the pan hot enough that the steak sizzles aggressively the moment it makes contact.
    3. 03
      Sear without moving
      Place the steak in the pan and don't touch it for 3–4 minutes. Resist the urge to move it. Flip once and sear the other side for another 3 minutes. For medium-rare, pull at an internal temp of 130°F (54°C).
    4. 04
      Butter baste
      Reduce to medium heat. Add the butter, garlic, thyme, and rosemary. Once foaming, tilt the pan and spoon the butter over the steak continuously for 1–2 minutes. This is what gives it the depth of flavour.
    5. 05
      Rest before slicing
      Transfer to a cutting board and rest for at least 5 minutes. Do not skip this. The juices redistribute as it rests. Slice against the grain and finish with a pinch of flaky salt.

    Cook's Notes

    The single biggest mistake is not getting the pan hot enough. If it's not visibly smoking, wait longer. A lukewarm pan steams the steak instead of searing it — you'll lose the crust entirely. Bone-in ribeyes (cowboy cuts) work beautifully here too; just add 2–3 minutes per side.
